# Fundamental: Artificial Intelligence

## 1. The Core Idea

Artificial Intelligence is the broad scientific and engineering discipline dedicated to creating machines or systems that can **simulate human intelligence**, performing tasks that typically require reasoning, learning, perception, and problem-solving.

_Analogy: If [[Fundamental - What is Machine Learning|Machine Learning]] is about teaching a computer to be a world-class salmon identifier, Artificial Intelligence is the entire field of marine biology—the broader quest to understand and replicate the entire aquatic ecosystem._

## 2. Why This is a Fundamental

AI is a cornerstone concept because it represents one of the ultimate goals of computer science and serves as the **parent field** for most modern data-driven disciplines. It provides the "why" for the "how" of its sub-fields.

- **The Overarching Goal:** Fields like Machine Learning, Robotics, and Natural Language Processing are not ends in themselves; they are methodologies and applications developed in the pursuit of the broader goal of AI. Understanding this context prevents a tool-centric view and encourages a problem-centric one.

- **A General-Purpose Technology:** Like the steam engine or the internet, AI is a foundational technology that is being integrated into nearly every other field of science, business, and art. Its principles are becoming a required literacy for understanding the next generation of technology in any domain.

- **Defines the Boundary of Computation:** The pursuit of AI constantly pushes the limits of what is computationally possible, driving innovation in hardware (GPUs, TPUs), software engineering ([[Fundamental - Cloud Computing|distributed systems]]), and our understanding of intelligence itself.


## 3. Key Details & Principles

The pursuit of AI has historically been divided into two main goals and several key approaches:

- **Narrow AI (ANI):** This is the AI we have today. It is designed to perform a single, specific task very well (e.g., playing chess, recognizing faces, translating languages). All modern commercial AI applications are Narrow AI.

- **General AI (AGI):** This is the hypothetical, future goal of creating an AI with the ability to understand, learn, and apply its intelligence to solve _any_ problem a human being can. It remains the subject of intense research and debate.


**Primary Sub-Fields & Disciplines:**

- **Machine Learning:** The most dominant and successful sub-field, focused on learning from data.
- **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** Enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.
- **Computer Vision:** Enabling computers to "see" and interpret visual information from the world.
- **Robotics:** Integrating AI with physical hardware to perceive and act upon the physical world.
- **Knowledge Representation & Reasoning:** The classic, symbolic approach to AI, focused on representing information in a logical way and reasoning about it (e.g., expert systems).

## 5. Connections to Other Fundamentals

AI is a meta-field that draws from and influences many other domains:

- **Child:** [[Fundamental - What is Machine Learning]] (ML is the primary _approach_ to achieving the _goal_ of AI).
- **Foundation:** [[Fundamental - Computer Science]] (Provides the theories of computation, algorithms, and data structures).
- **Inspiration:** **Cognitive Science & Neuroscience** (Provides models of how the human brain and intelligence work).
- **Ethical Framework:** **Philosophy & Ethics** (Raises critical questions about consciousness, bias, autonomy, and the societal impact of intelligent systems).
