### 1. Understanding the Limits

Machine learning systems are powerful but have clear limitations. They do not possess true understanding or reasoning; instead, they rely on patterns learned from data.

---

### 2. Dependence on Data

Machine learning models are only as good as the data they are trained on:

* Biased data leads to biased models
* Incomplete data limits generalization
* Poor-quality data degrades performance

Models cannot correct flaws in the data on their own.

---

### 3. Lack of Common Sense

Machine learning systems lack human common sense:

* They cannot reason beyond learned patterns
* They struggle with unfamiliar situations
* They do not understand context the way humans do

This limits their reliability in open-ended environments.

---

### 4. Generalization Boundaries

Models perform well within the scope of their training data but often fail when:

* Data distribution changes
* Inputs differ significantly from training examples
* The environment evolves over time

This is known as limited generalization.

---

### 5. Interpretability Challenges

Many machine learning models, especially deep learning models:

* Operate as “black boxes”
* Are difficult to interpret or explain
* Make it hard to understand why decisions were made

This can be problematic in high-stakes applications.

---

### 6. Ethical and Social Concerns

Machine learning introduces risks such as:

* Bias and unfairness
* Privacy concerns
* Misuse of automated decision-making

Responsible design and oversight are required.

---

### 7. Human Oversight

Machine learning systems require human involvement to:

* Define objectives
* Monitor performance
* Interpret results
* Make final decisions when necessary

They are tools, not autonomous decision-makers.

---

### 8. Key Takeaways

* Machine learning does not truly “understand”
* Data quality and bias strongly affect outcomes
* Generalization is limited to learned patterns
* Human judgment remains essential
